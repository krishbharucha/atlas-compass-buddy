University of Washington — Academic Policies & Regulations
==========================================================

GRADING SYSTEM
--------------
The University of Washington uses a numerical grading system ranging from 0.0 to 4.0:
- 4.0 = Excellent
- 3.0 = Good
- 2.0 = Satisfactory (minimum for graduation requirements in most programs)
- 1.0 = Below Standard
- 0.0 = Failing

Grade point values for letter grades:
A = 4.0, A- = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B- = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C- = 1.7, D+ = 1.3, D = 1.0, D- = 0.7, F = 0.0

A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 is required for graduation from any undergraduate program. Some programs, particularly in engineering and the sciences, require a higher GPA in major courses.

ACADEMIC PROBATION
------------------
A student is placed on academic probation if their cumulative GPA drops below 2.0 at the end of any quarter. Students on probation must:
1. Meet with their academic advisor within the first two weeks of the following quarter.
2. Enroll in no more than 15 credits per quarter while on probation.
3. Raise their cumulative GPA to 2.0 or above within two consecutive quarters.

Failure to meet probation requirements may result in academic suspension. A student who has been suspended may petition for reinstatement after sitting out for at least one full academic year.

CREDIT LOAD
-----------
- Full-time enrollment: 12–18 credits per quarter.
- Overload (19+ credits): Requires advisor approval and a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0.
- Minimum for financial aid: 12 credits per quarter for most federal and institutional aid.
- Summer quarter: Students may enroll in up to 18 credits across both sessions.

ADD/DROP DEADLINES
------------------
- Adding courses: Through the end of the 2nd week of instruction (no instructor signature needed for open courses).
- Adding courses (late): Weeks 3–7 require instructor and department approval, and a late-add fee of $20.
- Dropping courses without a "W": Through the end of the 6th week of instruction. No record on transcript.
- Dropping courses with a "W": Weeks 7–8. A "W" (Withdrawal) appears on the transcript but does not affect GPA.
- After week 8: Withdrawal requires a petition to the Registrar and is only granted for extraordinary circumstances (medical emergency, family crisis).

REPEAT POLICY
-------------
Students may repeat a course in which they received a grade below 2.0. Both grades appear on the transcript, but only the most recent grade is counted in the cumulative GPA. A course may be repeated a maximum of two times. After two repeats, the student must petition the department for further attempts.

INCOMPLETE (I) GRADES
---------------------
An Incomplete (I) grade may be assigned when a student has completed the majority of coursework satisfactorily but cannot complete the final portion due to circumstances beyond their control. The student must:
1. Request the Incomplete from the instructor before final grades are submitted.
2. Complete remaining work within the following quarter.
3. If work is not completed, the Incomplete converts to 0.0 automatically.

HONORS AND DEAN'S LIST
-----------------------
Students with a quarterly GPA of 3.5 or above and enrolled in at least 12 graded credits are placed on the Dean's List. Graduation honors are:
- Cum Laude: Cumulative GPA of 3.50–3.74
- Magna Cum Laude: Cumulative GPA of 3.75–3.89
- Summa Cum Laude: Cumulative GPA of 3.90–4.00

TRANSFER CREDITS
----------------
Transfer credits from regionally accredited institutions are accepted subject to departmental equivalency review. A maximum of 90 quarter credits may transfer from community colleges. Transfer grades are not included in the UW GPA calculation but do count toward total credits for graduation.

DOUBLE MAJORS AND MINORS
-------------------------
Students may declare a double major or add minors with the approval of each department. All requirements for each major/minor must be satisfied independently. A minimum of 30 unique credits must apply to each major (courses cannot double-count between two majors unless both departments agree in writing).
