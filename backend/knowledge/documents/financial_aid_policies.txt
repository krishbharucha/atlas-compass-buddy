University of Washington — Financial Aid Policies & Deadlines
==============================================================

FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid)
-------------------------------------------------
All students seeking financial aid must complete the FAFSA each academic year. Key deadlines:
- Priority filing deadline: January 15 each year for the upcoming academic year (Autumn, Winter, Spring).
- Final deadline: June 30 of the award year.
- Corrections and updates: Can be submitted until September 30 of the award year.

Students who miss the priority deadline may still receive federal aid but will have reduced access to institutional grants and work-study positions.

TYPES OF FINANCIAL AID
----------------------
1. **Federal Pell Grant**: Need-based; up to $7,395 per year (2025-2026). Does not need to be repaid. Eligibility based on Expected Family Contribution (EFC) from FAFSA.

2. **Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)**: For students with exceptional financial need; $100–$4,000 per year. Limited funding — priority filing is essential.

3. **UW Husky Promise**: Covers tuition and standard fees for Washington state residents whose family income is at or below 65% of the state median income (approximately $72,000 for a family of four). Renewable each year provided the student:
   - Maintains a 2.0 cumulative GPA.
   - Completes 30 credits per academic year.
   - Files the FAFSA by the priority deadline.

4. **Washington College Grant (WCG)**: State-funded grant covering tuition for families earning up to 100% of the state median income (~$110,000 for a family of four). Automatically considered when FAFSA is filed.

5. **Federal Work-Study**: Part-time employment for students with financial need. Jobs available on campus and with approved off-campus employers. Students earn at least the state minimum wage ($16.28/hr for 2025-2026). Maximum earnings are capped based on financial need.

6. **Federal Direct Loans**:
   - Subsidized: For students with demonstrated financial need. No interest accrues while enrolled at least half-time.
   - Unsubsidized: Available regardless of need. Interest accrues from disbursement.
   - Annual limits: $5,500 (freshmen), $6,500 (sophomores), $7,500 (juniors/seniors).
   - Aggregate limit: $31,000 for dependent undergraduates.

SATISFACTORY ACADEMIC PROGRESS (SAP)
-------------------------------------
To maintain financial aid eligibility, students must meet SAP requirements reviewed at the end of each quarter:
1. **GPA Requirement**: Maintain a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0.
2. **Credit Completion Rate**: Complete at least 67% of all attempted credits (including withdrawals and incompletes).
3. **Maximum Timeframe**: Complete degree within 150% of the published credit requirement (e.g., 270 attempted credits for a 180-credit degree).

Students who fail SAP are placed on Financial Aid Warning for one quarter. If SAP is not met after the warning quarter, aid is suspended. Students may appeal if there were extenuating circumstances.

DISBURSEMENT SCHEDULE
---------------------
Financial aid is disbursed approximately 10 days before the start of each quarter:
- Autumn 2025: September 14, 2025
- Winter 2026: January 1, 2026
- Spring 2026: March 20, 2026
- Summer 2026: June 14, 2026

Aid is first applied to tuition and fees. Any remaining balance is refunded to the student via direct deposit or check within 3–5 business days.

SCHOLARSHIP POLICIES
--------------------
- Departmental scholarships: Applied for through individual departments, usually with a February 1 deadline.
- UW General Scholarship Application: Opens November 1, closes February 15. One application covers hundreds of UW-specific scholarships.
- Outside scholarships: Must be reported to the Office of Student Financial Aid. They may reduce loan amounts but not grants.

EMERGENCY FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE
-------------------------------
The Student Emergency Aid Fund provides up to $1,500 per academic year for students facing unexpected financial hardship (medical bills, housing displacement, family emergency). Applications are reviewed within 48 hours. Students can apply through the Dean of Students office or at uw.edu/emergencyaid.

TUITION AND FEES (2025-2026)
------------------------------
- Resident undergraduate tuition: $3,386 per quarter (full-time).
- Non-resident undergraduate tuition: $13,155 per quarter (full-time).
- Student fees (technology, activities, services): ~$390 per quarter.
- Course fees for lab-intensive courses: $25–$200 depending on the course.

PAYMENT DEADLINES
-----------------
Tuition payment is due by the 3rd Friday of each quarter. Late payment incurs a $75 late fee. Failure to pay by the end of the 2nd week may result in course cancellation and a hold on the student's account.
