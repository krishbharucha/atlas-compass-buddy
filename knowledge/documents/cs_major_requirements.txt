University of Washington — Computer Science Major Requirements
===============================================================

ADMISSION TO THE CSE MAJOR
----------------------------
The Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) major is a capacity-constrained program. Students apply for direct admission as freshmen or through the competitive application process:

- Direct Admission: Offered to a limited number of incoming freshmen based on holistic review (GPA, essays, extracurriculars). About 80 students admitted directly each year.
- Competitive Admission (most common): Students apply after completing prerequisite courses. Two application cycles per year:
  - Autumn cycle: Apply by April 1. Decisions by May 15.
  - Spring cycle: Apply by November 1. Decisions by December 15.

Prerequisites for application:
1. CSE 142: Computer Programming I (minimum 3.2)
2. CSE 143: Computer Programming II (minimum 3.2)
3. MATH 124: Calculus I (minimum 2.5)
4. MATH 125: Calculus II (minimum 2.5)
5. Minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0.

Admission is competitive. Typical admitted student GPA in prerequisites: 3.6–3.9.

DEGREE REQUIREMENTS (BS in Computer Science)
----------------------------------------------
Total credits required: 180 quarter credits.

**Core CS Courses (52 credits):**
- CSE 311: Foundations of Computing I (4cr)
- CSE 312: Foundations of Computing II (4cr)
- CSE 331: Software Design & Implementation (4cr)
- CSE 332: Data Structures & Parallelism (4cr)
- CSE 333: Systems Programming (4cr)
- CSE 341: Programming Languages (4cr)
- CSE 351: Hardware/Software Interface (4cr)
- CSE 369: Introduction to Digital Design (3cr)
- CSE 401: Introduction to Compiler Construction (4cr)
- CSE 421: Introduction to Algorithms (4cr)
- CSE 451: Introduction to Operating Systems (4cr)
- CSE 452: Introduction to Distributed Systems (4cr)
- CSE Senior Capstone: CSE 403 (Software Engineering Capstone, 5cr)

**Math & Science Foundation (38 credits):**
- MATH 124, 125, 126: Calculus sequence (15cr)
- MATH 208: Matrix Algebra with Applications (3cr) — minimum grade C (2.0)
- STAT 311 or CSE 312 equivalent for probability requirement (4cr)
- PHYS 121, 122, 123: Physics sequence (15cr)
- One approved lab science elective (1cr from PHYS labs or CHEM labs)

**General Education & Electives (60 credits):**
- Arts & Humanities (VLPA): 15 credits from approved list.
- Social Sciences (I&S): 15 credits from approved list.
- Natural Sciences (NW): Fulfilled by physics sequence above.
- Additional breadth: 30 credits of free electives.

**Writing (15 credits):**
- English Composition (C courses): ENGL 111, 121, or 131 (5cr).
- Writing-intensive (W courses): Two W-courses, at least one in CSE (e.g., CSE 390W: Technical Writing in CS, or another approved W course). Total 10cr of W courses.

**Diversity Requirement (5 credits):**
- One 5-credit course from the approved Diversity list (e.g., AES 150, AMER 230, GWSS 200).

**Technical Electives (10 credits):**
- Two additional CSE courses numbered 300+, or approved courses from EE, INFO, or MATH.

SPECIALIZATION TRACKS (Optional)
----------------------------------
Students may declare a specialization (not required for graduation):
1. **Artificial Intelligence**: CSE 446, CSE 447, CSE 473, CSE 490 (ML)
2. **Systems & Networking**: CSE 452, CSE 461, CSE 462
3. **Human-Computer Interaction**: CSE 440, CSE 441
4. **Data Science**: CSE 414, CSE 446, STAT 390
5. **Security & Privacy**: CSE 484, CSE 490 (Security)

Each specialization requires 3–4 designated courses. Students declare through the CSE advising office.

ACADEMIC STANDING IN THE MAJOR
--------------------------------
Once admitted, students must maintain:
- Cumulative GPA of 2.0 or above.
- A grade of 2.0 or above in each required CSE course. Courses passed below 2.0 must be retaken.
- Satisfactory progress: Complete at least 36 credits per academic year to stay on track for 4-year graduation.

Students who fall below these standards receive a warning from the CSE advising office. Two consecutive quarters below standard may result in removal from the major.
