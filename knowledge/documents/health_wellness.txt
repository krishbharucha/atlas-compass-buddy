University of Washington — Student Health & Wellness Resources
==============================================================

HALL HEALTH CENTER
------------------
Hall Health Center (located on campus at 4060 George Washington Lane NE) provides primary care, mental health counseling, immunizations, and pharmacy services for all registered UW students.

Hours of operation:
- Monday–Friday: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
- Saturday: 9:00 AM – 1:00 PM (urgent care only)
- Sunday: Closed

Appointments can be made online at hallhealth.uw.edu or by calling (206) 543-5030. Walk-in appointments are available for urgent concerns but wait times may be 1–2 hours.

IMMUNIZATION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------
All students must provide proof of the following immunizations before registering for their second quarter:
1. Measles, Mumps, Rubella (MMR) — 2 doses.
2. Meningococcal conjugate (MenACWY) — 1 dose after age 16.
3. Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis (Tdap) — 1 dose within the last 10 years.
4. COVID-19 — Up-to-date per CDC guidelines.
5. Tuberculosis screening — Required for students from high-prevalence countries.

Students who do not submit immunization records by the deadline will have a registration hold placed on their account, preventing enrollment in future quarters.

COUNSELING CENTER
-----------------
The UW Counseling Center provides free, confidential mental health services to currently enrolled students.

Services include:
- Individual therapy (up to 12 sessions per academic year).
- Group therapy programs (anxiety management, grief support, identity exploration).
- Crisis counseling (same-day appointments available for students in distress).
- Psychiatric medication management (referral from a counselor required).
- Let's Talk drop-in sessions: Brief, informal conversations with a counselor. No appointment needed.
  - Odegaard Library: Tuesdays 1:00–3:00 PM
  - HUB: Wednesdays 10:00 AM–12:00 PM
  - Ethnic Cultural Center: Thursdays 2:00–4:00 PM

To schedule an appointment, call (206) 543-1240 or visit counseling.uw.edu. Initial consultations are scheduled within 5 business days. If you are in crisis, call the 24/7 Crisis Line at (206) 543-1240 (press 1) or text HOME to 741741.

HEALTH INSURANCE
-----------------
UW requires all students to have health insurance. Students without coverage are automatically enrolled in the UW Student Health Insurance Plan (SHIP):
- Premium: $867/quarter (2025-2026).
- Coverage includes: Outpatient visits, hospitalization, prescription drugs, mental health, vision, and dental basics.
- Waiver: Students with comparable coverage may waive SHIP by September 15 each year. Waiver forms are available at uw.edu/ship.

RECREATION AND FITNESS
-----------------------
The Intramural Activities (IMA) Building offers fitness facilities free to all registered students:
- Weight rooms, cardio equipment, basketball courts, swimming pool, climbing wall.
- Hours: Monday–Friday 6:00 AM – 11:00 PM; Weekends 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
- Group fitness classes: Yoga, spin, kickboxing — free with student ID.
- Intramural sports leagues: Registration at rec.uw.edu. Seasons run each quarter.

DISABILITY RESOURCES
---------------------
Disability Resources for Students (DRS) coordinates academic accommodations:
- Apply at drs.uw.edu with documentation of disability.
- Common accommodations: extended test time (1.5x or 2x), note-taking services, accessible seating, alternative format materials.
- Students are responsible for delivering accommodation letters to instructors each quarter.
- DRS is located in Mary Gates Hall, Suite 011. Phone: (206) 543-8924.

FOOD RESOURCES
--------------
The UW Food Pantry (located in Poplar Hall) provides free groceries to any student experiencing food insecurity. No proof of need required. Hours: Tuesday–Thursday 12:00–5:00 PM. The Any Hungry Husky program also provides emergency meal vouchers redeemable at campus dining locations — available through the Dean of Students office.

STUDENT EMERGENCY FUND
-----------------------
Students experiencing unexpected financial hardship can apply for emergency assistance of up to $1,500 per academic year. Common reasons include:
- Unexpected medical expenses not covered by insurance.
- Housing displacement (fire, eviction, domestic violence).
- Loss of income affecting ability to pay for basic needs.
Applications are reviewed within 48 hours. Apply at uw.edu/emergencyaid or visit the Dean of Students office in Schmitz Hall.
