University of Washington — Housing & Residential Life
=====================================================

RESIDENCE HALLS
---------------
The University of Washington operates eight residence halls on the Seattle campus:
1. McMahon Hall — Suite-style rooms for first-year students. Includes dining.
2. Haggett Hall — Traditional doubles. Closest to the HUB and libraries.
3. McCarty Hall — Traditional doubles and triples. Most affordable on-campus option.
4. Hansee Hall — Historic building with small doubles. Quiet community.
5. Lander Hall — Single rooms available for upper-division students.
6. Terry Hall — Single and double rooms. Integrated with conference facilities.
7. Maple Hall — Suite-style for second-year students and above.
8. Willow Hall — Newest hall (opened 2023). Full kitchenettes in each suite.

HOUSING APPLICATION
-------------------
- Application opens: March 1 each year for the following academic year.
- Priority deadline: May 1. Applications received by this date are guaranteed housing placement.
- Late applications: Accepted after May 1 on a space-available basis. Wait-list assignments begin mid-August.
- Housing deposit: $300, due upon application. Refundable (minus a $50 processing fee) if cancelled before June 15.

ROOM AND BOARD RATES (2025-2026)
----------------------------------
- Traditional double: $3,800/quarter
- Suite-style double: $4,200/quarter
- Single room: $4,800/quarter
- Meal plans are mandatory for first-year residents:
  - Level 1 (12 meals/week + $150 dining dollars): $1,650/quarter
  - Level 2 (unlimited meals + $50 dining dollars): $2,100/quarter
  - Level 3 (7 meals/week + $300 dining dollars): $1,450/quarter

Upper-division students may opt out of meal plans.

ROOMMATE MATCHING
-----------------
First-year students complete a lifestyle questionnaire covering sleep habits, study preferences, noise tolerance, and cleanliness expectations. The Housing office uses this data to match roommates. Students may also request a specific roommate by entering each other's NetID on the housing application before May 1.

HOUSING CONTRACT TERMS
-----------------------
The housing contract runs for the full academic year (Autumn through Spring). Early termination is permitted only for:
1. Graduation or academic dismissal.
2. Marriage or domestic partnership.
3. Medical reasons (with documentation from Hall Health or a licensed provider).
4. Approved study abroad program.

Students who break their contract without an approved reason are liable for the remaining quarters' room charges and forfeit their deposit.

QUIET HOURS AND COMMUNITY STANDARDS
------------------------------------
- Courtesy hours: 24/7. Residents are expected to keep noise at a reasonable level at all times.
- Quiet hours: Sunday–Thursday 10:00 PM to 8:00 AM; Friday–Saturday 12:00 AM to 10:00 AM.
- Finals week: Quiet hours are 24/7 during the last week of instruction and finals week.

Violations are addressed through the Residential Education conduct process:
1. First offense: Written warning and conversation with Resident Advisor.
2. Second offense: Meeting with the Residence Hall Director; possible probation.
3. Third offense: Potential removal from on-campus housing.

GUEST POLICY
------------
Overnight guests are permitted for a maximum of 3 consecutive nights, and no more than 6 nights per month. All guests must be signed in by the host resident at the front desk. Guests under 18 must have written parental permission on file. Roommate consent is required for any overnight guest.

MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS
------------------------
Non-emergency maintenance requests (broken fixtures, HVAC issues, pest control) can be submitted through the MyHFS portal at myuhfs.uw.edu. Response time is typically 2–5 business days. Emergency maintenance (flooding, fire alarm, lock-out) should be reported to the hall front desk immediately or by calling UW Facilities at (206) 543-0440, available 24/7.

MOVING IN / MOVING OUT
-----------------------
- Move-in day for Autumn quarter: Third Saturday in September. Check-in times are assigned by hall and floor.
- Move-out day at end of Spring quarter: Within 24 hours of the student's last final exam, or by the Saturday after finals week, whichever is earlier.
- Express checkout: Students may leave their keys in the room and complete an online checkout form. A $50 charge applies if any damage is found and the student is not present for inspection.
